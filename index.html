Coherent Topological Flow: Reconciling Safety and Geometry in Generative Robot Planning

Anonymous Author(s)

Abstract

Generative flow matching has emerged as a powerful paradigm for robot motion planning, yet ensuring safety in topologically complex environments remains challenging due to \textit{topological tearing}—a phenomenon where point-wise safety filters disrupt the trajectory's geometric continuity at topological decision boundaries. To address this, we introduce \textit{Coherent Topological Flow} (CTF), a framework leveraging a novel \textit{spectral-kernel duality} to reconcile hard safety constraints with the intrinsic connectivity of trajectory. CTF constructs a spectral precision matrix to enforce geometric stiffness against high-frequency tearing, complemented by a dual diffusion kernel that propagates local safety constraints globally. Furthermore, we propose a Magnitude-Direction Decoupling strategy grounded in the Vector Heat Method to guide the flow as a unified elastic body, preventing destructive interference. Theoretical analysis proves that CTF guarantees asymptotic safety, while experiments validate that our framework successfully reconciles strict safety constraints with topological consistency.
